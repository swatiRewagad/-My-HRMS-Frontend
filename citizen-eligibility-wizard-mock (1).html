Before you begin

Let's check if we can help — it takes about a minute

The RBI Ombudsman handles complaints about a deficiency in service by banks and other regulated organisations. A few quick questions will tell you whether your complaint is ready to file today, or whether there's a step to complete first. You can still file either way — this just saves you time.

Question 1 of 4

Who is your complaint about?

Choose the organisation your complaint is against.

Question 2 of 4

Have you already complained to them directly?

RBI can step in only after you've raised the matter with the organisation itself and given them a chance to resolve it.

Question 3 of 4

When did you complain to them, and did they reply?

This tells us whether the waiting period is complete.

Use the date on your complaint or acknowledgement.

Question 4 of 4

What best describes your complaint?

The RBI Ombudsman looks into a specific failure in service — not general feedback or a company's business decision. This helps us point you the right way.
